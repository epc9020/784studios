SOME GUY TO USE NUCLEAR BOMBS AS FIREWORKS THIS NEW YEAR

Reports indicate that a man identified as Hal Calhaun is planning to repurpose several actual nuclear bombs stored in his basement into fireworks for the upcoming New Year's celebration.

"This year, I don't just want to use any firework," Hal explained confidently. "This year I want to be the one guy on the block with the biggest, baddest, most kick-ass firework ever launched in human history."

The "firework" in question: a Mark 17 nuclear bomb, designed at Los Alamos National Laboratory in 1954. Only about 200 MK-17s were ever built. Hal possesses five of them because, according to him, "some previous homeowner must've left them behind or whatever."

Originally designed to be dropped from a Convair B-36 'Peacemaker'—an aircraft Hal does not own—he has instead mounted the bombs onto several rocket engines he does inexplicably own.

"What I did was just attach the bombs to these rockets I conveniently had laying around," Hal said, gesturing to the improvised launch platform. "I also strapped a couple hundred normal fireworks to the sides, like backup dancers. For flair."

When asked about the detonation issue, Hal was ready. "Now you might say, 'Hal, aren't these things designed to explode on impact with the ground?' And yes, absolutely. That's why I modified them to explode remotely!" Hal added, proudly displaying a homemade remote detonator built from what appeared to be a garage-door opener and duct tape. "Trust me, I know what I'm doing—my father was a nuclear engineer. Or so I heard."

At press time, Hal prematurely launched one of the armed nuclear "fireworks." Police responded immediately, charging him with unlawful noise and issuing a $700 fine. Officials added that "further charges may be pending, depending on whether or not that thing lands somewhere."
